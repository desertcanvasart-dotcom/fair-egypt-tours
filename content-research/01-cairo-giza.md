## Attractions

### Grand Egyptian Museum (GEM)  [local photo] — HEADLINE
On the Giza plateau ~2km from the pyramids, now the largest archaeological museum dedicated to a single civilisation. It opened to the public in November 2025 and finally gives the full Tutankhamun collection a home where all of it can be shown together for the first time. The building is part of the experience — a vast stone-clad atrium, a grand staircase lined with statues, and tall windows that frame the pyramids. For most visitors this is the single best reason to come to Cairo right now.
- Don't miss: the complete Tutankhamun galleries — the gold mask, nested coffins, jewellery, chariots and thousands of everyday objects shown together as one collection.
- Good to know: open daily ~9am–6pm, extended evenings Wed & Sat. Entry is timed and must be booked online in advance (no on-site sales, daily cap). Budget 4–5 hours; an early slot beats the crowds in the Tut rooms.

### Pyramids of Giza & the Great Sphinx  [local photo]
The pyramids of Khufu, Khafre and Menkaure and the Sphinx that guards them are ~4,500 years old and still the reason most people first think of Egypt. They sit on a desert plateau at the edge of the city, so you can stand at the base of a structure built before the wheel was common in Egypt and see modern Cairo behind you. Busy and hot, but it lives up to the picture in your head.
- Don't miss: going inside the Great Pyramid of Khufu — a narrow climbing passage to the empty burial chamber. Tickets are limited; book ahead.
- Good to know: plateau open ~7am–4pm (later in Ramadan). General entry ~700 EGP (~$15); inside Khufu costs extra. Card payment at most gates. Budget 2–3 hours, go early.

### Saqqara & the Step Pyramid of Djoser  [local photo]
The huge ancient burial ground for Memphis, ~30 min south of Giza and far quieter than the main plateau. Its centrepiece is the Step Pyramid of Djoser, the oldest large stone building in the world and the design that led to the smooth-sided pyramids. The site also holds painted tombs, fresh discoveries, and the Serapeum, an underground gallery built for sacred bulls. Pairs well with Giza the same day.
- Don't miss: the Serapeum, a long rock-cut tunnel lined with two dozen enormous granite sarcophagi, each carved from a single block.
- Good to know: open ~8am–5pm (last entry ~4pm). Combined ticket ~450 EGP (~$9–10) covers the Step Pyramid grounds and Imhotep Museum. Budget 2–3 hours.

### Citadel of Salah al-Din (Saladin)  [local photo]
A medieval fortress begun by Saladin in the 1170s on a rocky hill above the city, the seat of Egypt's rulers for nearly 700 years. From its walls you get the widest free view over Cairo, and on a clear day you can pick out the pyramids. Inside sit several mosques — the alabaster-clad Muhammad Ali Mosque and its tall twin minarets are the landmark you can spot from much of the city.
- Don't miss: stepping into the Muhammad Ali Mosque — huge domed ceiling, hanging lamps, cool marble courtyard.
- Good to know: open daily ~8am–5pm (last entry ~4pm). Entry ~550 EGP (~$12). The mosque closes during prayer times; dress modestly (shoulders & knees). Budget 2 hours.

### Khan el-Khalili bazaar & Islamic Cairo  [local photo]
A sprawling market in the heart of medieval Cairo, with covered lanes trading since the 1300s. Around it spreads Islamic Cairo — historic mosques, old gates and tea houses where ordinary life still goes on. You come here to wander, haggle over lamps, spices and silver, then sit with a mint tea and watch the street.
- Don't miss: a tea at El Fishawy, a narrow, mirror-lined café serving in the same alley for well over two centuries.
- Good to know: free to wander, busiest late afternoon and evening; Friday mornings are quieter. Expect to bargain, keep small notes handy, budget 2–3 hours. Comfortable shoes for the uneven lanes.

## Hotels (4)

### Pyramids View Inn
- Area: Nazlet El-Samman, Giza, on Sphinx Street facing the plateau · 2–3★ / Value · ~$35–55/night
- Description: A small, long-running guesthouse a short walk from the entrance gate, with simple rooms and a rooftop that looks straight at the Sphinx and pyramids. You trade fancy facilities for one of the best views in Giza and a breakfast served with the pyramids in front of you.
- Amenities: A/C, en-suite w/ hot water, private pyramid-view balcony, free WiFi & satellite TV, rooftop breakfast included, free airport pickup

### Marvel Stone Hotel
- Area: Al-Haram / Nazlet El-Samman, Giza, across from the pyramids entrance · 4★ / Comfort · ~$45–70/night
- Description: A well-reviewed mid-range hotel close to the plateau, with tidy modern rooms and a rooftop restaurant that takes in a wide pyramid panorama. A comfortable step up from the budget guesthouses while keeping prices fair and the views front and centre.
- Amenities: A/C, flat-screen satellite TV, private bath w/ mini-fridge & safe, rooftop pyramid-view restaurant, Egyptian breakfast, 24h reception & room service, airport transfers & tour desk

- Amenities: free WiFi, daily breakfast, restaurant & bar, terrace lounge w/ city views, 24h front desk, A/C rooms w/ private bath
