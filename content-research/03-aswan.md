## Attractions

### Philae Temple (Temple of Isis, Agilkia Island)  [local photo]
Built for the goddess Isis, one of the last places where the old Egyptian religion was practised, well into the Roman era. When the Aswan dams threatened to drown it, a UNESCO project cut the whole thing into thousands of blocks in the 1960s–70s and rebuilt it on higher Agilkia Island. You reach it by a short motorboat ride; the carvings of Isis, Hathor and Horus are still crisp, and the columns feel intimate next to the giant temples further north.
- Don't miss: the evening sound-and-light show, when the colonnades and pylons are lit and the story of Isis plays across the stone.
- Good to know: the only way over is by boat from Shellal landing — agree the fare before you step aboard.

### Unfinished Obelisk  [local photo]
A single obelisk still lying in the granite bedrock of an ancient quarry, abandoned partway through carving when a crack appeared. Finished, it would have stood ~42m and weighed close to 1,000 tons — larger than any obelisk ever raised. Because work stopped, you see exactly how the masons cut into the rock. A short, flat visit.
- Don't miss: the wooden walkways that let you look down the length of the obelisk and spot the crack that ended the project.
- Good to know: almost no shade and the granite throws back heat — go early, carry water.

### Aswan Botanical Garden (Kitchener's Island)  [local photo]
A whole small island in the Nile planted as one long garden, with trees and shrubs from Africa, Asia and beyond. Given to Lord Kitchener in the 1890s, it is one of the calmer, greener stops in Aswan — shaded paths between palms and flowering trees while feluccas drift past.
- Don't miss: the central avenue of royal palms, which frames the river at each end.
- Good to know: reachable only by boat, usually combined with a felucca trip; best in late afternoon when the heat eases.

### Elephantine Island  [local photo]
The largest island in the Nile at Aswan and one of the oldest inhabited spots in the area, with ruins back to the earliest dynasties. It holds the Temple of Khnum, an ancient nilometer that measured the flood, and the small Aswan Museum. Painted Nubian villages sit alongside the archaeological zone, so you get ancient stone and everyday life on one walk. No cars.
- Don't miss: the nilometer steps cut down to the water, where markings once tracked the flood and set the year's taxes.
- Good to know: a public ferry crosses cheaply from the Corniche — no private boat needed.

### Nubian Village  [local photo]
The Nubian villages around Aswan are known for bright painted houses, sand streets and a culture distinct from the rest of Egypt. Many families welcome visitors for tea, a meal, or to see crafts and music — a relaxed, friendly stop to learn about Nubian history and the displacement caused by the High Dam.
- Don't miss: a home-cooked Nubian lunch, often served on a rooftop or courtyard over the river.
- Good to know: arrive by camel or motorboat depending on the village; bring small cash for tea, henna and handmade goods.

## Hotels (4)

### Basma Hotel Aswan
- Area: Aswan town, on the hill next to the Nubian Museum
- Stars/Category: 4★ / Comfort · ~$90–110/night
- Description: Basma sits on high ground above town, so many rooms look over the Nile and greenery. A dependable mid-range base with a garden, a pool and an easy walk to the Nubian Museum, without resort prices.
- Amenities: outdoor pool & deck, terraced garden, restaurant & poolside bar, free breakfast buffet, free WiFi, free parking, spa & massage

### Pyramisa Isis Island Aswan Resort & Spa
- Area: Isis Island, a private island in the Nile (boat shuttle)
- Stars/Category: 5★ / Comfort resort · ~$110–140/night
- Description: Takes up its own green island in the Nile, reached by a short boat shuttle, so every room looks onto water, gardens or the hills. A roomy, quiet retreat with several pools and restaurants — good for families and couples wanting space away from town.
- Amenities: private island & shuttle, two outdoor pools + heated pool, four restaurants, spa w/ sauna & Turkish bath, tennis & squash, kids' playground, free WiFi, free parking

### Aswan Plaza Hotel (formerly Citymax Aswan)
- Area: Corniche al-Nil, central Aswan, riverfront
- Stars/Category: 4★ / Comfort value · ~$55–80/night
- Description: Central riverfront hotel best known for its rooftop pool, where you can watch the sun set over the Nile. Rooms are simple and modern, some with river views, steps from the Corniche and the souk. (Renamed Aswan Plaza in early 2026; same property as the former Citymax.)
- Amenities: rooftop Nile-view pool, fitness centre, sauna & jacuzzi, restaurant & 24h coffee shop, rooftop café, free WiFi, free parking

### Bob Marley Guest House
- Area: Elephantine Island, by the ferry crossing
- Stars/Category: guesthouse (~2–3★) / Value · ~$20–35/night
- Description: A small, family-run Nubian guesthouse on Elephantine Island, with simple A/C rooms, a sun terrace and its own restaurant. About as affordable and local as Aswan gets, a short ferry hop from the Corniche with the river right outside.
- Amenities: A/C rooms w/ private bath, sun terrace, restaurant & bar (Nubian food), tea/coffee in room, free WiFi, ferry access
