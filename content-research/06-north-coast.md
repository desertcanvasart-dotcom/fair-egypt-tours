## Attractions

### El Alamein War Cemeteries & Museum  [WEB IMAGE]
The 1942 Battle of El Alamein turned the tide of the North African campaign, and the evidence sits quietly along the coast ~130km west of Alexandria. The Commonwealth War Cemetery holds more than 7,000 graves in neat rows above the sea, with separate German and Italian memorials nearby. The military museum next door lays out tanks, field guns, uniforms, maps and personal effects across open-air yards and indoor halls. A sober, moving stop that breaks up the beach time with real history.
- Don't miss: the visitor register books inside the Commonwealth cemetery, with names and messages left by visiting families.
- Good to know: open daily ~07:00–17:00 (cemetery), ~09:00–16:00 (museum); museum entry is cheap. Go in the morning before the heat — much of it is outdoors. Dress modestly.
- Web image: https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/f/f1/ElAlameinCommonwealthWarCemetery.jpg

### Agiba Beach (Marsa Matruh)  [WEB IMAGE]
Agiba means "miracle," and the name fits this small cove ~24km west of Marsa Matruh. You reach the sand by a footpath down from the clifftop; the reward is clear turquoise water trapped between limestone walls, with natural caves cut into the rock. Busy in peak summer and almost empty the rest of the year. No lifeguards — swim with care.
- Don't miss: the view from the clifftop café before you climb down, framing the whole cove and the colour of the water.
- Good to know: bring water, shade and footwear for the rocks; few facilities on the sand, so stock up in Marsa Matruh first.
- Web image: https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/6/64/Agiba_Beach_-_Marsa_Matrouh_-_Egypt.JPG

### Cleopatra Beach & Cleopatra's Bath (Marsa Matruh)  [WEB IMAGE]
A short drive from Marsa Matruh town, known for dramatic rock formations standing out of the shallows. Cleopatra's Bath is a natural rock pool that fills with seawater, wrapped in legend that Cleopatra and Mark Antony once bathed here. A walkway leads out over the rocky shoreline. More about scenery and photos than a long swim, since the rocks and surge make open swimming tricky.
- Don't miss: the rock pool at calmer hours, when the water sits still enough to see straight through it.
- Good to know: the rocks get slippery and the sea can surge into the bath — keep your footing, don't climb out onto the formations in rough conditions.
- Web image: https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/8/89/Cleopatra%E2%80%99s_Beach_in_Marsa_Matrouh_city_in_Egypt_on_the_northern_coast_of_the_Mediterranean.JPG

### New Alamein City  [WEB IMAGE]
Egypt's purpose-built coastal city, opened in 2018 and still growing, with landmark towers, an opera house, museums and universities behind the beach. The headline for visitors is the ~14km seafront promenade lined with restaurants, cafés, a cinema, beach clubs and a large concert venue. Same clear Mediterranean blue as the wilder beaches, but with full services and nightlife. Modern and planned rather than old-world.
- Don't miss: the promenade at sunset, when the beach bars and seafront restaurants come alive.
- Good to know: a summer-season destination — liveliest Jun–Sep, much quieter off-season. Check what's open before a winter trip.
- Web image: https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/6/61/New_Alamein_1.jpg

### Sidi Abdel Rahman & Ras El Hekma Beaches  [WEB IMAGE]
West of New Alamein, Sidi Abdel Rahman has long been the postcard stretch: powder-white sand and water from pale green to deep blue, anchored by resort communities like Marassi. Keep driving toward Ras El Hekma, roughly halfway between Alexandria and Marsa Matruh, for one of the longest stretches of clear, undeveloped Mediterranean coast — now a huge new development but still beautiful for its coves and dunes. The best of the coast for simply lying on calm, clean water.
- Don't miss: the shallow turquoise lagoons at Sidi Abdel Rahman, calm and clear enough for easy swimming.
- Good to know: many beaches sit inside private resort compounds or gated communities — access can depend on where you stay.

### Al Alamein Hotel
- Area: Sidi Abdel Rahman (Marassi bay) · 4★ / Resort · ~$105–150/night
- Description: A historic bayfront resort open since 1960, right on the calm turquoise water of Sidi Abdel Rahman. Rooms range from garden views to direct beach access, with pools, a spa and several restaurants.
- Amenities: private beach w/ beach-access rooms, outdoor & kids pools, spa, fitness & yoga, four restaurants, sea-view rooms
- Note: families and married couples only; couples may be asked for a marriage certificate at check-in.

### Porto Matrouh Beach Resort
- Area: Marsa Matruh · 4★ / Comfort-Resort · ~$60–140/night
- Description: An affordable, family-friendly resort with sea-view balconies on every room and a relaxed pool setup. A solid budget base for exploring Agiba and Cleopatra beaches.
- Amenities: outdoor & children's pool, private beach (off-site, shuttle), restaurant & bar, garden, playground, free parking
- Note: private beach is a short shuttle from the resort; heavily summer-seasonal.

### Porto Marina Resort & Spa Al Alamein
