# Siwa Oasis — research dossier

> No local photos — all 5 attractions use Wikimedia images (stable). Note: Siwa is remote — ~4h from Marsa Matruh, 8–10h from Cairo.

Intro: Siwa sits deep in Egypt's Western Desert near the Libyan border. It's a long haul: ~4h drive from Marsa Matruh, or 8–10h from Cairo. The distance is part of why Siwa still feels like its own world, with its own Berber language and culture. Most people come for a few unhurried days of springs, salt lakes, mudbrick ruins and desert.

## Attractions

### Shali Fortress  [WEB IMAGE]
The old town of Siwa, a tangle of mudbrick and salt-clay (kershef) buildings lived in for centuries until heavy rains in 1926 melted much of it away. You can walk up through the ruins to the top, where the views open out over the palm groves and desert. Best in the late afternoon, when the low sun turns the walls gold and the heat eases. The newer town wraps around the base.
- Don't miss: climbing to the highest point for sunset over the oasis.
- Good to know: uneven surface, no railings — wear proper shoes and watch your footing near the edges.
- Web image: https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/d/d3/SiwaShaliEastSide.jpg

### Temple of the Oracle of Amun (Aghurmi)  [WEB IMAGE]
The temple where, in 331 BC, the priests are said to have confirmed Alexander the Great as the son of Amun. It sits on a rocky outcrop in the old village of Aghurmi, a few km from the town centre. Not much signage — read up beforehand or bring a guide — but the setting and history carry it. The climb up gives a good look across the palms toward the salt lakes.
- Don't miss: the inner sanctuary where the oracle was consulted, still partly intact.
- Good to know: bring small cash for the ticket; go early before the midday heat.
- Web image: https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/7/74/Siwa_Oasis%2C_Temple_of_the_Oracle_of_Amun%2C_Egypt.jpg

### Cleopatra's Spring (Cleopatra Bath)  [WEB IMAGE]
A round, stone-rimmed natural pool fed by a constant flow of clear spring water, set among the palm groves on the road toward the Oracle temple. The name is tradition rather than fact, but it's the most popular bathing spot in the oasis and people swim here year-round. A small café beside it for tea afterward. Busy in the afternoons.
- Don't miss: an early-morning swim before the tour groups arrive.
- Good to know: a public spot — modest swimwear is the respectful choice, especially for women.
- Web image: https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/a/a5/Cleopatra_bath_Siwa00.jpg

### Salt lakes and pools  [WEB IMAGE]
Bright turquoise pools cut into white crystalline banks, a short drive from town. The water is so salty you float without effort, the way you would in the Dead Sea, and the minerals leave your skin feeling clean. The colour contrast between blue water and white salt is the main draw. Several pools are open to swimmers, easy to reach by car or bike.
- Don't miss: floating on your back in the middle of a pool with the salt banks all around you.
- Good to know: salt stings any cut — cover small scrapes and rinse off with fresh water afterward; keep it out of your eyes.
- Web image: https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/3/33/Siwa_Salt_pools05.jpg

### Great Sand Sea & Bir Wahed  [WEB IMAGE]
West of town the palms give way to the Great Sand Sea, a vast field of dunes crossed by 4x4 on a half-day safari. The trip usually pairs sandboarding with a stop at Bir Wahed, a hot spring on the desert edge warm enough to soak in after dark, with a cold freshwater pool nearby. Drivers time the loop for sunset from the top of a dune. The desert highlight most visitors remember best.
- Don't miss: the hot-spring soak at Bir Wahed as the sun goes down over the dunes.
- Good to know: a military-controlled zone — entry only with a licensed local driver, so book the safari through your lodge or a registered operator.
- Web image: https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/0/02/SiwaBirWahid.jpg

## Hotels (4)

### Adrère Amellal
- Area: lakeside at the foot of Adrère Amellal mountain, ~18km from town · Eco-lodge / Luxury · ~$550–650/night (often full-board)
- Description: The famous one — a kershef lodge built entirely from local salt-rock and mud, lit by beeswax candles instead of electricity. A quiet, off-grid splurge with an organic garden, a spring-fed pool, and rooms that each look different.
- Amenities: spring-fed pool, organic farm-to-table dining, desert excursions, lakeside setting, candlelit rooms (no electricity), full-board option

- Source: https://www.adrereamellal.com/adrere/

### Taziry Ecovillages
- Area: edge of the oasis near the salt lakes · Eco-lodge / Boutique (3★) · ~$150–250/night (seasonal)
- Description: A larger eco-village of palm and mudbrick chalets, some carved into the hillside, set around a natural spring-fed pool. Keeps the rustic look but adds real comforts like hot water and power — an easier sell than the fully off-grid lodges.
- Amenities: spring-fed pool, spa, restaurant, private verandas, horse & camel rides, 4x4 desert safaris
- Images: ⚠ site-hosted, no fixed direct URL — pull from page
- Source: https://taziry.com/

### Kenooz Shali Lodge
- Area: central Siwa town, in the palm groves below the old Shali fortress · Eco-lodge / Value (3★) · ~$50–90/night (B&B)
- Description: A mudbrick village lodge in the traditional kershef style, with 20 simple rooms, palm-shaded terraces, and a small pool under the trees. The affordable way to get the heritage Siwan feel without the eco-lodge price tag, walkable to everything in town.
- Amenities: palm-grove pool, restaurant w/ Siwan dishes, terraces over the groves, traditional furnishings, central location
- Images ✅:

  - https://siwa.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/06/Shali-Lodge-Entrance-1.jpg
- Source: https://siwa.com/lodges/

### Palm Trees Hotel
- Area: central Siwa town, ~9-min walk from Shali fortress · Budget (2★) / Value · ~$15–30/night
- Description: The reliable budget pick right in the centre, with plain rooms, a few garden bungalows, and a shady palm garden. Basic but clean and friendly, walkable to the springs, restaurants and the bus stop.
- Amenities: palm garden, bar, free parking, continental breakfast, central location, ground-floor bungalows
