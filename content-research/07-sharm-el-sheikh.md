## Attractions

### Ras Muhammad National Park  [WEB IMAGE or local Red Sea coral]
At the southern tip of Sinai, where the Gulf of Aqaba meets the Gulf of Suez, Ras Muhammad protects some of the best reef in the Red Sea. The walls drop straight into deep blue water, so you swim alongside coral busy with hundreds of fish species, and the famous Shark and Yolanda reefs sit just offshore. About 40 minutes from town, as a boat trip or from the shore. Snorkellers and divers both get plenty out of a day here.
- Don't miss: the Yolanda reef wall, where the reef falls into open water and the coral is at its most dramatic.
- Good to know: protected park with an entry fee; bring your passport for the checkpoint. Best conditions Oct–Apr.
- Web image: https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/2/2f/Coral_reef_in_Ras_Muhammad_nature_park.JPG

### Naama Bay  [WEB IMAGE]
The lively heart of Sharm — a curved beachfront strip of hotels, cafés, dive shops and a pedestrian promenade that fills up after dark. An easy place to spend a relaxed day: swim in the morning, walk the seafront in the evening, eat somewhere different each night. Everything is within walking distance, good for first-timers and families. Prices run a little higher than the Old Market.
- Don't miss: the evening stroll along the promenade when the lights come on.
- Good to know: central and walkable — stay here without renting a car or relying on taxis.
- Web image: https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/5/51/Naama_Bay_R01.jpg

### Tiran Island & the Straits of Tiran  [WEB IMAGE]
The Straits of Tiran hold four big reefs named after the cartographers who charted them: Jackson, Woodhouse, Thomas and Gordon. Boats run out from Sharm for a full day of snorkelling or diving over coral known for clear water and strong colour. The current can be lively, which is part of why the marine life is so good. One of the most popular day trips on the coast.
- Don't miss: Jackson Reef, the northernmost, with the wreck of a cargo ship still visible above the surface.
- Good to know: boat-only, usually a full day with lunch on board — bring sun cover and motion-sickness tablets if prone.
- Web image: https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/c/cc/Tiran_R01.jpg

### Old Market (Sharm El Maya)  [local photo]
The part of Sharm that feels most like a working Egyptian town rather than a resort strip. Narrow lanes packed with spice stalls, lamps, cotton and small cafés, with a large mosque at its heart. The place to buy souvenirs and eat well for less than Naama Bay. Haggling is expected and friendly.
- Don't miss: the El Sahaba Mosque, mixing Ottoman and Mamluk styling, lit up in the evening.
- Good to know: prices are negotiable; liveliest after sunset when it cools down.

### Mount Sinai & St Catherine's Monastery (sunrise day trip)  [WEB IMAGE or local Sinai shots]
An overnight trip rather than a quick excursion: leave Sharm late evening, drive a couple of hours into the mountains, climb Mount Sinai in the dark to reach the summit for sunrise. The view across the bare Sinai peaks as the light comes up is the reason people do it. On the way down you visit St Catherine's Monastery, one of the oldest working monasteries in the world, built where Moses is said to have seen the burning bush.
- Don't miss: the sunrise from the 2,285m summit, after the final ~750 stone steps.
- Good to know: ~14-hour round trip with a real climb — bring warm layers, water and proper shoes; a camel can carry you up part of the path.
- Web image: https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/6/6a/Sunrise_from_the_summit_of_Mount_Sinai_or_Gabal_Musa.jpg

## Hotels (4)  — all image URLs verified live

### Sharm Holiday Resort
- Area: Hadaba / Um El Sid (short walk to Naama Bay) · 4★ / Value · ~$60/night (all-inclusive, double)
- Description: A straightforward all-inclusive that keeps the cost down without cutting the basics, with its own beach and pools a few minutes from the Naama Bay promenade. Good for travellers who want sun, sea and full board at a fair price.
- Amenities: private beach, outdoor pool, two restaurants & three bars, health club w/ sauna, tennis & squash, dive/snorkel desk, free WiFi

### Naama Bay Hotel & Resort (formerly Tropitel Naama Bay)
- Area: Naama Bay (on the promenade) · 5★ / Comfort · ~$85/night (half-board, double)
- Description: A central beachfront resort right on the Naama Bay strip, so the cafés, dive shops and nightlife are all on your doorstep. The aqua park and several pools make it an easy pick for families who want to stay in the middle of things.
- Amenities: four pools + three-slide aqua park, private Naama Bay beach, spa & fitness, dive/water-sports centre, several restaurants & poolside bar, daily entertainment

### Reef Oasis Beach Resort (house reef)
- Area: Hadaba, on Temple Reef · 4–5★ / Resort · ~$130/night (all-inclusive, double)
- Description: Built around its own house reef — the one to choose if snorkelling and diving straight off the beach is the point of the trip. A full dive centre on site plus plenty of pools, dining and shade.
- Amenities: own house reef (Temple Reef), on-site dive centre, multiple pools, six restaurants & bars, spa, private sandy beach, all-inclusive
